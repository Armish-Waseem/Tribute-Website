Abdul Sattar Edhi

1928–2016

The Angel of Mercy

Abdul Sattar Edhi (February 28, 1928 – July 8, 2016) was a renowned Pakistani humanitarian and philanthropist, often called the "Angel of Mercy." He founded the Edhi Foundation in 1951, which became Pakistan's largest social welfare organization, running hospitals, orphanages, homeless shelters, and the world's largest volunteer ambulance network. Edhi's tireless dedication to helping the needy, regardless of their background, earned him global recognition and numerous awards. Despite his vast humanitarian impact, he lived modestly, reflecting his commitment to selflessness and compassion. His legacy lives on through the continued work of the Edhi Foundation.

Achievements

  • Founded the Edhi Foundation in 1951.
  • Established the world's largest ambulance network.
  • Provided free health care, education, and shelter to millions.
  • Received numerous awards, including the Gandhi Peace Award and the Lenin Peace Prize.
